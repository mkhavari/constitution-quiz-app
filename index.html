While the U.S. Constitution was signed on September 17, 1787, it did not go into effect until nine of the 13 states had ratified it. The ninth state to do so was New Hampshire on June 21, 1788.

The first ten amendments to the U.S. Constitution are known as the Bill of Rights. The Preamble is the introduction to the U.S. Constitution. The Articles of Confederation was the constitution which bound the thirteetn states prior to the ratification of the U.S Constitution. The Gettysburg Address is a famous speech given by President Abraham Lincoln during the American Civil War, during a dedication of a soliders' cemetery after the Battle of Gettysburg.

The First Amendment protects freedom of the press, freedom of speech, freedom of religion, freedom to peaceably assemble, freedom to petition the government for a redress of grievances. Slavery was still legal in the United States when the First Amendment was adopted. Also, at that time, the only people allowed to vote in the United States were property-owning white men. The right to bear arms is protected by the Second Amendment, not the First.

Slavery in the United States was abolished by the Thirteenth Amendment. However, the Thirteenth Amendment makes an exception allowing for involuntary servitude as a punishment for a crime.

The U.S. Constitution was last amended in 1992. It prohibits any law altering the salary of members of Congress from taking effect until the start of the next set of terms for members of the House of Representatives. The amendment was submitted to the states for ratification on September 25, 1789, but was not ratified by enough states until May 7, 1992, setting a record ratification period of 202 years, 7 months, and 12 days.
